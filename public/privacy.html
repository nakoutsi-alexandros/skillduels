The short version

Skill Duels gives you an account automatically, without asking for your email, your name, or a password. All it keeps is a random ID, the nickname you pick, your chosen avatar, and your Season Points score. Your nickname shows on the public leaderboard — that is the point of it. We do not track you, run ads, or sell anything about you. You can wipe your account whenever you want.

The rest of this page explains that properly, because the law says we have to.

2. Your account and what it holds

The first time you open Skill Duels, the app quietly creates an account for you. It does not ask for an email, a password, a phone number or your real name. Instead it generates a random ID number and remembers it, so that when you come back your nickname and score are still there.

The complete list of what your account holds is:

WhatWhat it is
A random IDA string of random characters (a UUID). It is not linked to your name, email or device identity. It is just a label so your data is yours.
Your nicknameThe name you pick. This is public — it appears on the leaderboard for other players to see. Please do not use your real full name; a nickname is all you need.
Your avatarWhich of the built-in avatar pictures you chose.
Your Season PointsYour score for the current season, so you can climb the leaderboard.

That is the whole footprint. There is no email, no password, no location, no contacts, no advertising ID, and no sensitive data of any kind.

A note about storage on your device

To keep you signed in to that same account between visits, the app stores a login token in your browser's local storage. This is strictly necessary to make the account work — without it, every visit would be a brand-new stranger. We do not use any cookies or storage for advertising or analytics.

Why we hold this and the legal basis

We process your account data to do one thing: run the game for you — remember who you are, save your score, and show the leaderboard you are playing to reach. Under the GDPR our lawful basis is Article 6(1)(b), performance of a contract: providing the game you asked to use. Your nickname being visible to other players is not a separate choice — it is built into the leaderboard feature you chose to take part in.

We do not use your data for profiling, advertising or any automated decision that affects you. There is still no analytics, no tracking pixel and no advertising SDK in the app.

The share button

If you tap "copy", we put a short summary of your run on your device's clipboard. That happens entirely on your device. We do not see it and nothing is sent to us. What you do with the copied text afterwards — pasting it into another app — is covered by that app's own privacy policy, not ours.

3. Where your data lives, and who processes it

We do not run our own servers. Two service providers act as our processors under GDPR Article 28, handling data only on our instructions:

  • Supabase — stores your account (the ID, nickname, avatar and Season Points above) and runs the sign-in. Our Supabase project is hosted in the European Union (Frankfurt, Germany), so this data stays in the EU. Reference: [[SUPABASE_DPA_REFERENCE]].
  • Vercel Inc. — serves the game's files (the page, fonts and images). Like every web server, it automatically writes a short technical log when your device requests a page: your IP address, the time, the file requested and your browser's user-agent string. Where data reaches the United States, the transfer relies on the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and the EU–US Data Privacy Framework.

We do not sell your data, we do not share it with advertisers, and no one else receives it.

5. Children

In Greece the age at which a young person can agree to an online service handling their data on their own is 15. So:

  • If you are 15 or older, you can use Skill Duels yourself.
  • If you are under 15, a parent or guardian should agree on your behalf before you make an account and pick a nickname.

We keep what a child's account holds to the same tiny list as everyone else's — a random ID, a nickname, an avatar and a score — and we never ask a child for their real identity. Please remember the nickname is public, so children should never use their real name. If you are a parent and want your child's account removed, write to [[CONTACT_EMAIL]] and we will delete it.

6. Your rights

Under GDPR you can ask us to:

  • tell you what data we hold about you (access);
  • correct it if it is wrong (rectification);
  • delete it (erasure);
  • restrict or object to how we use it;
  • give you a copy in a portable format;
  • where we ever rely on consent, withdraw that consent at any time.

Deleting your account really deletes it. When your account is deleted, we remove your random ID from the sign-in system, and your nickname, avatar and Season Points are deleted along with it automatically. Nothing about you is kept, and your nickname leaves the leaderboard. This is a true erasure, not a hidden "disabled" flag.

To delete your account or exercise any other right, email [[CONTACT_EMAIL]] and we will action it. We answer within one month. [[DELETE_UI_STATUS_NOTE]]

One honest limitation: because we deliberately do not know your name or email, we may not be able to connect a request to a specific account unless you are contacting us from within the app or can identify your account to us. If we genuinely cannot tell which data is yours, we will tell you rather than guess (GDPR Art. 11).

7. Security

The site is served over HTTPS and your data sits in a managed EU database (Supabase) protected by row-level security rules, meaning each account can only reach its own data. The best protection, though, is how little there is: no email, no password and no real identity to steal. Your nickname is public by design; treat it as public.
